## Example 1: SaaS Product Strategy (Executive Audience, 10 Slides)

This example demonstrates a Persuasion Arc for an internal strategy presentation.

---

### Slide 1 — AI-Powered Customer Intelligence: Our Next Growth Lever

**Purpose:** Frame the strategic opportunity and establish urgency.

**Content:**
- Customer churn increased 18% YoY despite NPS remaining stable
- Current analytics surface what happened — not what will happen
- Predictive customer intelligence closes this gap and unlocks $4.2M ARR

**Visual Recommendation:** Single large metric — "18% churn increase" — with trend line showing the divergence between NPS (flat) and churn (rising).

**Key Takeaway:** Our current tools are measuring satisfaction while missing risk signals.

**Transition:** Let's examine what's driving this blind spot.

---

### Slide 2 — The Insight Gap

**Subtitle:** Why satisfied customers are still leaving

**Purpose:** Establish the complication — the core problem that demands action.

**Content:**
- NPS captures sentiment at a single point in time
- Usage patterns, support ticket frequency, and feature adoption tell a different story
- 73% of churned accounts showed declining engagement 60+ days before cancellation
- We have the data — we're not using it

**Visual Recommendation:** Split slide — left side shows NPS scores (steady), right side shows engagement metrics (declining) for the same accounts. The visual mismatch tells the story.

**Key Takeaway:** The signals exist. We need the system to detect them.

**Speaker Notes:** Pause on the 73% stat. Ask the room: "How many of those accounts could we have saved with a 60-day head start?" Let them do the mental math.

**Transition:** Here's what predictive intelligence looks like in practice.

---

### Slide 3 — Predictive Customer Intelligence: How It Works

**Purpose:** Present the solution architecture at a level executives can follow.

**Content:**
- Ingests usage analytics, support data, billing patterns, and CRM signals
- ML models identify risk patterns 60–90 days before churn
- Surfaces prioritized risk accounts to CSMs with recommended actions
- Learns from outcomes — improves with every save and every loss

**Visual Recommendation:** Horizontal data flow diagram: Data Sources (4 icons) → AI Engine → Risk Dashboard → CSM Actions → Outcome Feedback Loop.

**Key Takeaway:** It's not another dashboard — it's an early warning system that tells your team exactly where to focus.

**Transition:** We've validated this approach with a controlled pilot.

---

### Slide 4 — Pilot Results

**Purpose:** Provide evidence that the approach works — reduce the perceived risk of the decision.

**Content:**
- 12-week pilot across 200 mid-market accounts
- Identified 34 at-risk accounts — 28 confirmed risk through CSM outreach
- Intervention saved 19 accounts ($380K ARR retained)
- False positive rate: 17% (acceptable, improving with more data)

**Visual Recommendation:** Four large metrics in a grid layout: "200 accounts monitored" | "34 risk alerts" | "19 saves" | "$380K retained." Accent color on the $380K.

**Key Takeaway:** 82% detection accuracy and $380K saved in 12 weeks — with a model that's still learning.

**Speaker Notes:** Anticipate the question: "What about the false positives?" Frame it: 6 false positives out of 34 alerts is a 17% rate. CSMs spent ~2 hours total on unnecessary outreach. The cost of a miss is 100x the cost of a false positive.

**Transition:** Let's talk about what full deployment looks like.

---

### Slide 5 — Full Deployment Plan

**Purpose:** Show a credible, phased implementation path.

**Content:**
- **Phase 1 (Q1):** Expand to all mid-market accounts, integrate with Salesforce
- **Phase 2 (Q2):** Add enterprise tier, build automated playbooks
- **Phase 3 (Q3):** Extend to expansion revenue prediction — not just churn prevention

**Visual Recommendation:** Three-phase horizontal timeline with milestones. Each phase shows accounts covered and expected ARR impact.

**Key Takeaway:** Phased rollout de-risks the investment while accelerating time-to-value.

**Transition:** Here's what we need to make this happen.

---

### Slide 6 — Investment Required

**Purpose:** Present the ask clearly — what it costs and what it returns.

**Content:**
- Total investment: $420K (engineering, data infrastructure, ML ops)
- Ongoing: $85K/year (cloud compute, model maintenance)
- Projected Year 1 ROI: 6.2x based on pilot extrapolation
- Break-even: Month 4 of full deployment

**Visual Recommendation:** Two-column layout — left column shows costs (stacked bar), right column shows projected returns (growing bar chart over 12 months) with break-even line marked.

**Key Takeaway:** $420K investment with a 4-month payback and 6x return.

**Transition:** Let's look at the risks.

---

### Slide 7 — Risks and Mitigations

**Purpose:** Demonstrate mature thinking — acknowledge risks before they're raised.

**Content:**
- **Data quality risk:** Incomplete CRM data → Mitigation: data hygiene sprint in Phase 1
- **Adoption risk:** CSMs ignore alerts → Mitigation: integrate into existing Salesforce workflow, not a separate tool
- **Model accuracy risk:** False positives erode trust → Mitigation: human-in-the-loop review for first 90 days

**Visual Recommendation:** Three-row table with columns: Risk | Impact (High/Med/Low) | Mitigation | Owner.

**Key Takeaway:** Every identified risk has an owned mitigation plan.

---

### Slide 8 — Competitive Landscape

**Purpose:** Show that not acting has consequences beyond churn.

**Content:**
- 3 of 5 direct competitors have launched predictive churn features in the last 18 months
- Gainsight and Totango offer this as add-on — our customers will expect it
- Building in-house gives us proprietary advantage tied to our specific product data

**Visual Recommendation:** Competitive positioning table: competitors listed with checkmarks for predictive features they offer. Our row shows the gap.

**Key Takeaway:** This is becoming table stakes. The question isn't whether — it's when.

**Transition:** Here's what I'm asking for today.

---

### Slide 9 — The Decision

**Purpose:** Make the ask explicit and binary.

**Content:**
- Approve Phase 1 funding ($180K) and engineering allocation (2 engineers, 1 data scientist)
- Timeline: Begin February, Phase 1 live by end of Q1
- Decision needed by January 15 to hit Q1 timeline

**Visual Recommendation:** Clean, minimal slide. Large text with the three ask items. No charts, no clutter. The simplicity signals "this is the moment."

**Key Takeaway:** $180K and 3 people to start. Full deployment decision comes after Phase 1 results.

**Speaker Notes:** This is a gated ask. You're not asking for $420K today — you're asking for $180K to prove Phase 1. The full commitment comes later with data. Frame it as low risk.

---

### Slide 10 — From Reactive to Predictive

**Purpose:** Close with the vision — what success looks like.

**Content:**
- Today: We learn about churn when it happens
- Tomorrow: We see it coming 60–90 days early and act
- Our goal: Move from a reactive retention model to a predictive growth engine

**Visual Recommendation:** Two-state illustration — left side shows "firefighting" (reactive), right side shows "radar" (predictive). Arrow connecting them labeled "This investment."

**Key Takeaway:** This isn't a tool purchase — it's a capability shift that changes how we grow.

---

## Why This Example Works

1. **Pyramid Principle applied:** The recommendation and evidence come early, not at the end
2. **SCR structure:** Situation (Slide 1) → Complication (Slide 2) → Resolution (Slides 3–8) → Ask (Slide 9)
3. **One idea per slide:** No slide tries to do two things
4. **Visual recommendations are specific:** "Horizontal bar chart comparing X and Y" not "add a chart"
5. **Speaker notes add value:** They provide delivery guidance, not slide restatement
6. **Transitions connect logically:** Each slide's transition leads naturally to the next
7. **The ask is concrete:** Not "let's discuss" but "$180K, 3 people, by January 15"
8. **Closes with vision, not logistics:** The final slide elevates from tactics to strategy
