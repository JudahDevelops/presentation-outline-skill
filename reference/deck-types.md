# Deck Type Reference

Structural templates for specific presentation types. Load this when the user requests a specific kind of deck.

---

## Business Proposal

**Narrative:** Persuasion Arc (SCR + Ask)
**Typical length:** 10–15 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Context | 1–2 | Establish shared understanding of the situation |
| Problem/Opportunity | 2–3 | Define the gap — what's wrong or what's possible |
| Proposed Solution | 3–4 | Your recommendation with approach details |
| Evidence | 2–3 | Case studies, data, proof points |
| Implementation | 1–2 | Timeline, resources, next steps |
| Ask | 1 | Specific approval, budget, or decision needed |

**Tone:** Confident, evidence-driven, action-oriented
**Visual emphasis:** Data charts, comparison tables, timeline graphics

## Investor / Pitch Deck

**Narrative:** Pitch Structure
**Typical length:** 10–12 slides (Guy Kawasaki's rule — never exceed 10 content slides)

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Title + Hook | 1 | One-sentence description of what you do |
| Problem | 1–2 | Pain with evidence |
| Solution | 1–2 | Your product/service — clear and specific |
| Product Demo | 1 | Screenshots, workflow, or architecture |
| Traction | 1 | Metrics, growth curves, customer logos |
| Market | 1 | TAM/SAM/SOM with credible sizing |
| Business Model | 1 | Revenue model, unit economics |
| Competition | 1 | Positioning map or differentiator table |
| Team | 1 | Key people, relevant experience |
| The Ask | 1 | Fundraise amount, use of funds, timeline |

**Tone:** Bold, concise, numbers-first
**Visual emphasis:** Growth charts, product screenshots, competitive positioning maps
**Font minimum:** 30pt (Kawasaki's rule — if you need smaller, you have too much text)

## Technical Architecture

**Narrative:** Technical Flow
**Typical length:** 12–18 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Context | 1–2 | Business problem and technical requirements |
| Current State | 2–3 | Existing architecture, limitations, pain points |
| Proposed Design | 3–5 | Architecture diagrams, component breakdown, data flow |
| Trade-offs | 1–2 | What you're optimizing for and what you're accepting |
| Implementation Plan | 2–3 | Phases, milestones, dependencies |
| Risk & Mitigation | 1 | Technical risks with mitigation strategies |
| Next Steps | 1 | Immediate actions, decisions needed |

**Tone:** Precise, honest about trade-offs, implementation-focused
**Visual emphasis:** Architecture diagrams, sequence diagrams, system topology, comparison tables

## Product Review / Project Update

**Narrative:** Status/Decision Arc
**Typical length:** 8–12 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Context | 1 | Project scope and objectives reminder |
| Progress | 2–3 | What was accomplished — metrics, milestones |
| Demos / Highlights | 1–2 | Show what was built or achieved |
| Challenges | 1–2 | Blockers, risks, lessons learned |
| Decisions Needed | 1–2 | Specific choices the audience must make |
| Next Steps | 1 | Timeline for upcoming work |

**Tone:** Transparent, metric-driven, decision-oriented
**Visual emphasis:** Progress dashboards, milestone timelines, RAG status indicators

## Sales Deck

**Narrative:** SCR + social proof
**Typical length:** 8–12 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 1 | Provocative statistic or question |
| Problem | 1–2 | Customer pain — make them nod |
| Solution | 2–3 | Your product solving their specific problem |
| Social Proof | 1–2 | Customer stories, logos, testimonials |
| Differentiators | 1 | Why you vs alternatives |
| Pricing / Packages | 1 | Clear options (if appropriate) |
| Next Steps | 1 | CTA — trial, meeting, pilot |

**Tone:** Customer-centric, benefit-focused, conversational
**Visual emphasis:** Customer logos, before/after comparisons, product screenshots

## Educational / Workshop

**Narrative:** Learning Arc
**Typical length:** 15–30 slides (depending on duration)

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 1 | Why this topic matters to the learner |
| Learning Objectives | 1 | What they'll know/do by the end |
| Foundation | 3–5 | Core concepts, definitions, frameworks |
| Deep Dive | 4–8 | Detailed exploration with examples |
| Practice / Exercise | 2–4 | Hands-on application (for workshops) |
| Synthesis | 1–2 | Connect the pieces — the big picture |
| Resources | 1 | Further reading, tools, references |

**Tone:** Approachable, example-rich, building-block progression
**Visual emphasis:** Diagrams, step-by-step illustrations, comparison tables, code snippets (for technical)

## Research Presentation

**Narrative:** Scientific method adapted
**Typical length:** 12–20 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Title + Context | 1–2 | Research question and why it matters |
| Background | 2–3 | Prior work, gap in knowledge |
| Methodology | 2–3 | How you investigated |
| Results | 3–5 | Key findings with data visualizations |
| Analysis | 2–3 | What the results mean |
| Implications | 1–2 | So what? Impact on the field |
| Limitations & Future Work | 1 | Honesty about scope and next steps |

**Tone:** Rigorous, evidence-first, measured
**Visual emphasis:** Data charts, statistical graphics, methodology diagrams

## Internal Strategy

**Narrative:** Pyramid Principle
**Typical length:** 10–15 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Recommendation | 1 | The strategic direction — stated upfront |
| Strategic Context | 2–3 | Market landscape, competitive dynamics, internal capabilities |
| Supporting Pillars | 3–5 | 2–4 key initiatives that support the recommendation |
| Resource Requirements | 1–2 | Investment, team, timeline |
| Expected Outcomes | 1–2 | Success metrics, projected impact |
| Risks | 1 | Key risks and mitigation |
| Decision / Ask | 1 | What needs to happen next |

**Tone:** Strategic, data-informed, decisive
**Visual emphasis:** Market maps, strategic frameworks (2x2 matrices), timeline roadmaps

## Executive Briefing

**Narrative:** Pyramid + BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)
**Typical length:** 5–8 slides

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Bottom Line | 1 | The recommendation or key finding — first |
| Context | 1 | Only what's necessary to understand the recommendation |
| Supporting Evidence | 2–3 | The strongest 2–3 data points |
| Implications | 1 | What happens if we act / don't act |
| Decision | 1 | Binary choice or specific approval needed |

**Tone:** Crisp, decisive, respect-the-audience's-time
**Visual emphasis:** Minimal — key metrics, simple charts, decision matrices

**Executive briefing rules:**
- Get to the point in slide 1, not slide 5
- No build-up, no suspense — executives have 10 minutes, not 10 slides of patience
- Every slide must earn its existence — if it doesn't change the decision, cut it
- Anticipate the top 3 questions and address them in the evidence section

## Conference Talk / Keynote

**Narrative:** Duarte Sparkline or Hero's Journey
**Typical length:** 15–25 slides (for 20–45 min talks)

| Section | Slides | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 1–2 | Open with story, question, or surprising fact |
| What Is | 2–3 | Current reality — establish the world |
| What Could Be | 2–3 | The possibility — contrast with reality |
| Tension | 3–5 | Alternate between problems and possibilities |
| Turning Point | 1–2 | The key insight or breakthrough |
| New Bliss | 2–3 | The transformed future — vivid and specific |
| Call to Action | 1 | What the audience should do next |

**Tone:** Inspiring, personal, story-driven
**Visual emphasis:** Full-bleed images, minimal text, dramatic contrasts, speaker-heavy (slides are backdrops)

**Keynote rules:**
- Maximum 6 words per slide. The talk is the content; slides are visual anchors.
- Use full-screen images, not clipart or stock photos with watermarks
- Personal stories > data for keynotes (data supports, stories convince)
- Build to a single memorable moment — the "takeaway sound bite"
